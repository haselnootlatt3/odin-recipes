Philippine Adobo

Time-old and iconic dish to share with the adobros

One of, if not one of the most popular dishes in the Philippines. The use of vinegar (or other acidic agents like calamansi juice) to preserve food had been present in pre-colonial Philippines and has since evolved over the centuries, remaining a beloved meal that's cooked at home, found at street markets, or at huge festivals. There are countless variations of adobo depending on region, or even the family making it. Variations of the dish include the use of coconut milk, chili peppers, and lemon grass. Ingredients

  • chicken (5 small wings; 5 small drumsticks)
  • 1/2 onion (jullienned)
  • brown sugar (1~3 Tbsp dependong on taste)

For the Marinade

  • soy sauce (100 ml)
  • ginger (30 ml) (sliced, julienned or grated) (30 ml)
  • garlic (2~3 cloves) (sliced, minced, or grated)
  • vinegar (50 ml) (apple cider vinegar; white vinegar; rice vinegar; calamansi juice)
  • water (300 ml)
  • bay/laurel lead (3~5 leaves depending on size)
  • peppercorns (15 ml) (or ground pepper)

Optional Additions

  • green onions (chopped)
  • chili peppers
  • boiled eggs
  • tomatoes

Preparation

  1. Combine marinade ingredients and chicken into ziploc bag (if u have chili peppers, add them here)
  2. Let marinade sit for at least 2 hours (best over night)

Cooking the Adobo

  1. Heat oil in a pan and fry meat first (try to brown the surface)
  2. After browning meat, pour in the marinade and add brown sugar (and boiled eggs if you have). Bring the sauce to a boil and simmer for about 15~20 min. Turn the meat/eggs around every so often, or ladle sauce on top of them.
  3. Add onions (and tomatoes if u have) and continue to let the sauce simmer for another 15~20 min. (You can cook it until the sauce gets really thin and oily, or if u like sauce, don’t cook it down too much.)
  4. Taste test and add more sugar if too salty (and add chopped green onions if u have some.)

Notes

  • Serve with rice or bread
  • You can switch out chicken for pork or beef ribs; and ofc you can use larger cuts of chicken, just make sure they all fit in ur pan/pot.
  • This isn't really a dish for chicken breast, but it's not impossible to use that type of meat, just that u'll have to cook the chicken breast a bit differently to avoid overcooking.
  • If you have leftover sauce, make some fried rice with it
